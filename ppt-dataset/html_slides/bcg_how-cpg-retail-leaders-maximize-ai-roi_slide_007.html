As agentic capabilities mature and as AI moves from decision support to workflow orchestration, the full-scale opportunity could expand close to 5% for CPG and retailers alike. This represents full potential delivered at scale through the use of future technology before appropriate time-scales.

Tapping some of the next value pools may depend on pre-competitive foundations. Several of the highest value opportunities identified cross-sector collaboration on infrastructure at immense economies of development. Surveyed executives are split on whether AI will create opportunities for voluntary collaboration, sentry to competition, or remain too uncertain to predict its future effects. The near-term opportunity may be to identify primary precompetitive foundational elements that help AI-enabled interactions work more reliably (for example, natural products data taxonomies, supply and stock signal definitions, and other standards that do not involve sharing competitively sensitive decisions).

Measurement remains the weak link. More than half of respondents said that they do not formally measure the ROI of consumer AI investments. (See Exhibit 4.) The most common reported barrier to scaling was that pilot economics did not translate into full-scale ROI.
