Preface

The projections included in this issue of the Fiscal Monitor are drawn from the same database used for the October 2024 World Economic Outlook and Global Financial Stability Report (and are referred to as "IMF staff projections"). Fiscal projections refer to the public (general government) sector unless indicated otherwise. The projections are based on officially announced budgets, adjusted for differences between the national authorities and the IMF staff regarding macroeconomic assumptions. The fiscal projections incorporated in the Fiscal Monitor are judged by the IMF staff as likely to be implemented. For countries supported by an IMF arrangement, the projections are those under the arrangement. For all other countries, the Fiscal Monitor evaluates policies to assess the sustainability of public finances, including budget intentions and prospects for policy implementation; an unchanged cyclically adjusted primary balance is assumed, unless indicated otherwise. Details on the composition of the groups, as well as country-specific assumptions, can be found in the Methodological and Statistical Appendix of the October 2024 Fiscal Monitor: Putting a Lid on Public Debt.

The Methodological and Statistical Appendix was prepared by Xunji Li under the guidance of Alejandra Sobarzo. Anna-Julia Poulliez, Yilin (Yi) Mao, and Gomez Diez from the Communications Department led the editorial team and managed the report's production, with editorial and production support from Michal Harrup, Lucy Scott Manda, Kerry Whipple, and Absolute Production Service, Inc., and The Sheridan Group.

The analysis and policy considerations are those of the IMF staff, drawing from contributions from area departments—namely, the African Department, Asia and Pacific Department, European Department, Middle East and Central Asia Department, and Western Hemisphere Department—and functional departments including the Fiscal Affairs Department, Institute for Capacity Development, Legal Department, Monetary and Capital Markets Department, Research Department, Secretary's Department, Statistics Department, and Strategy, Policy, and Review Department. Chapter 1 of the Fiscal Monitor also benefited from comments by Tobias Broer (Paris School of Economics), Michèle Cavallo (Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve), Henrike Lindh, Jochen Mankart (DIW Berlin), Uja Panizza (Geneva Graduate Institute), and Eric Paraules Herrero (Inter-American Development Bank) at the IMF workshop on "Putting a Lid on Public Debt" in July 2024.

Both projections and policy considerations are those of the IMF staff and should not be attributed to workshop participants, Executive Directors, or to their national authorities.
