Executive summary
European tech M&A is recalibrating — deal volume stabilizes while quality premiums widen
1
Deal volume has stabilized after a two-year correction, with European tech transactions expected to rise 8–12% in 2025 — driven by mid-market consolidation in SaaS, fintech, and cybersecurity
2
Valuation multiples are bifurcating: high-growth, profitable businesses command 12–18× EBITDA while undifferentiated assets face compression to 5–8×, creating significant selection risk for acquirers
3
Strategic acquirers are outbidding PE in AI-adjacent assets by 15–25%, as corporations pursue capability gaps; PE is pivoting to carve-outs and add-ons where synergies are pre-negotiated
4
Cross-border complexity has increased: EU screening frameworks now cover 18 member states, adding 3–7 months to close timelines — deal teams must price regulatory risk at term sheet stage
5
Value creation emphasis has shifted post-acquisition: 72% of surveyed acquirers name operational transformation (not multiple expansion) as the primary return driver for 2025–2027 hold periods

Our recommendations
Three strategic priorities for 2025 — focus on quality assets, regulatory preparation, and operational value creation
1
Tighten target screening around profitability, not just growth
In a bifurcated market, undifferentiated growth assets will continue to reprice downward. Prioritize EBITDA-positive businesses with defensible ARR and clear path to margin expansion.
2
Embed regulatory timelines into deal economics from day one
FDI screening and sector-specific review can add 4–8 months to close. Model this into IRR assumptions and build regulatory contingency clauses into LOIs.
3
Shift value creation planning pre-LOI, not post-close
With multiple expansion constrained, operational transformation must be scoped and resourced before signing. Integration costs built into pricing protect against post-close erosion.
