At a more granular level, in one group of countries, including Australia, Korea, and New Zealand, inflation was still substantially above target in late 2023, owing in part to sustained price pressures from services. In a second group—consisting of emerging markets and Japan—headline inflation has returned close to central bank target ranges and core inflation has been broadly contained. In a third group of countries, notably China and Thailand, falling commodity prices—and, in the case of Thailand, the extension of energy subsidies—have pushed consumer price inflation into negative territory, while core inflation has remained positive but is low.

Note: In panel 1, core is CPI excluding food and energy. Fuel and transport: the asset categories used in the decomposition of CPI correspond to the ILO Classification of Individual Consumption by Purpose (COICOP) categories. Asia EMDE includes China, India, Indonesia, Malaysia, the Philippines, Thailand, and Vietnam. Rest of the world AE includes Australia, France, Korea, New Zealand, the Netherlands, Sweden, Switzerland, the United Kingdom, and the United States.
