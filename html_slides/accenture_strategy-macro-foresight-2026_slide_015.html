Canada
  • Slight improvement vs. 2025 as the drag from past rate hikes fades,
    though growth remains US demand-dependent and pressured by tariffs
  • Structural challenges—productivity, housing supply, limited fiscal room—
    keep Canada in the lower-growth tier of the global multi-speed landscape
  • How quickly monetary easing transmits through a debt-heavy household sector
  • Outcome of USMCA renegotiations and any easing in tariffs
  • Signs of housing-market stabilization vs. renewed stress
Brazil
  • Decelerating overall growth as tighter financial conditions and high real
    rates weigh on domestic investment and consumer demand
  • Weaker global goods trade and China’s export overhang will also pressure
    Brazil’s manufacturing and commodity-linked sectors
  • Interaction between rate cuts and inflation expectations amid currency volatility
  • Exposure to China’s demand for metals, agri., and energy
  • Execution of fiscal reforms and market reactions
Mexico
  • Slight growth pickup supported by nearshoring, remittances, and a firm
    labor market, but still constrained by weak investment, softer US/global
    demand, and tariff headwinds
  • Tight financial/fiscal conditions—high real rates, limited fiscal room,
    energy-sector bottlenecks—will temper benefits of disinflation
  • USMCA review and tariff decisions shaping nearshoring economics and export competitiveness
  • Banxico’s easing path and effects on credit and peso volatility
  • How fiscal and energy-sector signals (Pemex risks, power reliability, budget plans) affect investor confidence
Saudi
Arabia
  • Slight acceleration of growth as oil production normalizes from prior
    OPEC+ cuts and non-oil activity remains resilient on the back of steady
    investment, job creation, and low inflation
  • Momentum is partly tempered by higher living costs (housing), subsidy
    adjustments, and a more cautious fiscal stance
  • Oil prices and OPEC+ quota decisions, the main swing factor for headline growth and fiscal space
  • Translation of committed public and private investments into actual project delivery and non-oil activity
  • Impact of subsidy reforms, housing costs, and public-sector wage dynamics on consumer sentiment
UAE
  • Growth is expected to moderate slightly from 2025’s strong pace as the
    multi-year non-oil surge—led by real estate, tourism, and services—
    normalizes, though it remains the primary driver of expansion.
  • Oil-sector growth turns modestly positive (from flatness in 2025) as
    OPEC+ output curbs ease and new ADNOC capacity comes online
  • Durability of non-oil activity, especially in real estate, tourism, and consumer credit
  • Progress on trade, investment, and digital-economy agreements that enhance the UAE’s hub role
  • Sensitivity to global financial conditions, oil prices, and regional geopolitics affecting capital flows and confidence
