1. Gradual
squeeze
  • Steady rise in Chinese high-tech exports, pricing pressure, but no major policy moves
  • Demand broadly stable
  • Trade policy steady, with China focused on mid-range cost competition
  • Tilt mix toward defensible niches and service-led offerings in exposed lines
  • Execute “no-regrets” efficiency moves (e.g., yield, procurement, basic China+1 options)
  • Build integrated scenarios by product and region, with early-warning triggers on profitability and cash
2. Selective
protection
  • Targeted tariffs and trade remedies in a few product areas offer partial shelter
  • US/EU apply sector-specific remedies
  • Retaliation remains contained
  • Reposition portfolio to benefit from protected segments where you have a right to win
  • Adjust footprint to capture local-content incentives in priority markets
  • Model tariff, subsidy and local-content scenarios into P&L, balance sheet and capex plans
3. Aggressive
undercutting
  • Visible dumping and deep price discounts in key products despite some protection
  • China sustains very low pricing via scale / support
  • Customers highly price-driven
  • Exit or shrink commodity SKUs
  • Double down on differentiated propositions and branding
  • Accelerate automation, site consolidation and supplier resets in loss-making lines
  • Reprioritise capex and M&A toward high-return, defensible positions
  • Put guardrails around “value-destructive defence” spending
4. Trade and
technology
shock
  • Broad tariffs / quotas
  • China moves up the tech stack into systems and software
  • Material escalation in tariffs / quotas
  • China credible in systems and software
  • Prioritise markets which can be defended
  • Differentiate on branding
  • Regionalise production (e.g., US / EU / APAC hubs)
  • Redesign networks for rules of origin
  • Conduct full balance-sheet stress tests for volume shocks and working-capital spikes
