Future normal retirement ages will continue to rise for both men and women, albeit at different rates

On average across all OECD countries and assuming labor market entry at age 22 in 2018, the normal retirement age will increase from 64.2 to 66.1 for men and from 63.5 to 65.7 for women

Currently, gender differences in the normal retirement age exist in around one-third of OECD countries but these will be mostly phased out after 2050 apart from a very small number of countries (Switzerland, Poland, Israel)

The economic crisis triggered by the coronavirus pandemic has heightened the strain on the future of national pension systems – billions of people now face working longer, or having less income in later life; governments will need to adopt a significant range of reforms to shore up long-term outcomes for future pensioners and their security in retirement
