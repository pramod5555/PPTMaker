All the risk we cannot see
With hindsight, all risks are obvious. Yet as we entered 2023, no one foresaw that a bank failure in California would escalate into a regional banking panic and ultimately lead to the merger of Switzerland's last two major banks.
Given the far-reaching consequences of events such as these, banks need to improve their planning for risks we cannot always see. This is especially true as stability continues to elude the industry and the markets it serves. In our latest Risk Survey, 72% of senior banking risk professionals said their organization's risk management capabilities and processes have failed to keep pace with the rapidly changing risk landscape.¹⁰
