Q6:
How do we move quickly without losing control of risks and costs?

Success starts with operational stability. Companies need to establish clear rules governing which actions AI systems can execute, which exceptions require escalation, and where human review is still mandatory. This is particularly important where AI intersects with consumer-facing applications, regulated activities, and supply chain commitments.

Companies should monitor cost inflation. As AI scales, token spending and model usage can become a significant recurring operating cost. The risk profile, however, is not uniform across AI transformations. At the deploy level, broad workforce access can create material costs at a time when productivity benefits remain diffuse, so leaders need to carefully track adoption and cost per utilization type and user group.

At the reshape level, the cost question is most acute in connection with AI and GenAI solutions for the IT function. Consequently, leaders need clear ROI data on reshape activities and a decisive view of where and how GenAI solutions will be used. For other functional areas, such as R&D, quality, and operations, AI and GenAI consumption costs are usually less prominent unless broad deployment or significant cost reduction is at stake. At the investment level, customer-facing propositions such as chatbots and AI agents require a strong business case that included value at stake, ROI, and leading KPIs for confirming, reducing, or expanding investment. Teams use the current workflow of the as-is baseline. Teams mapped the current workflow, quantified the distribution of time and effort across the workflow, and used that information as a baseline for gauging expected improvement.

Honest engagement with the workforce is critical. Leaders should communicate early on about how work will change, which tasks will be automated, and what new capabilities teams will need. Deploying autopilot first to manual, repeatable work gives teams a chance to experience AI as a way to reduce toil before it begins changing higher-stakes work.
