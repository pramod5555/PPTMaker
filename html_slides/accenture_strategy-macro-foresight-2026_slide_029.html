Organizations already embedding AI across core workflows
Strong capabilities, use cases, and growing returns
•  Redesign for end-to-end workflow •  Enable greater agility and adaptability within people and process to disruption
•  Differentiating AI-enabled services across the streams e.g., –  Propositions –  Pricing •  Realise higher productivity as AI maturity advances
•  New revenue models enabled by AI (e.g., personalised services) prioritisation
•  Emphasis on governance as models grow more complex •  Best positioned to leverage agentic AI within existing process
Organizations with meaningful AI opportunities but uneven progress
Selective adoption and barriers e.g., regulatory, capability or data restraints
•  Prioritise the highest-value use cases to avoid fragmented adoption •  Seamless shift towards standardised decision workflows
•  Scale priority initiatives while driving targeted innovation ahead of competitors
•  Investments with areas of visible ROI to avoid experimental sprawl •  Is there enough financial evidence to support scaling decisions?
•  Address data quality, access and barriers
Organizations where AI adoption remains limited due to operational constraints, legacy systems or low applicability
•  Focus on foundational digital and process improvements –  Specific areas (e.g., reporting) –  Improving data literacy
•  Risk of losing market share if foundations are not strengthened
•  Early investment in data systems
•  Minimum-viable data governance to enable AI use •  Modernisation of legacy platforms
