> The old-age dependency ratio
  is the number of people aged
  65+ (people who are typically
  not in the labor force) per 100
  people aged 20-64 (people
  who are typically in the labor
  force). For example, in Europe
  in 2050, 100 working-age
  people will have to support 54
  people aged 65+, up from 32
  people aged 65+ in 2020

> A high or increasing old-age
  dependency ratio can be a
  burden as it causes high(er)
  expenditure on pensions,
  health and care

> However, this ratio ignores that
  people above the age of 65
  are not necessarily dependent
  on support as a rising
  proportion is in work; reversely
  not all of those considered of
  working age are actually in
  work
